# Detec — Brand Foundation

The single reference document for what Detec is, who it's for, and why it exists. Read this first before the voice guide, color system, or any collateral.

---

## Purpose

Detec discovers and controls autonomous AI tools on developer endpoints. We give security teams honest, evidence-based visibility into what is running so they can govern proportionally instead of guessing or blocking blindly.

## Vision

A world where autonomous AI tools on developer endpoints are adopted confidently because governance keeps pace with capability. Security teams trust their tooling because it shows its work.

## Mission

Discover and control autonomous AI tools by what they do, not what they're called. Score every detection with explainable confidence. Enforce policy proportionally. Publish known limits.

## Values

### Evidence over assertion
Every claim Detec makes — to operators, to buyers, to the market — is traceable to specific signals, lab results, or documented methodology. If we can't show the evidence, we don't make the claim.

### Proportional governance
Governance is a spectrum, not a switch. Low-risk tools get visibility. High-risk tools get gates. The operator's policy defines the thresholds, not ours.

### Earned trust
We publish known limits, evasion test results, and honest gaps. Transparency about what we can't do builds more trust than perfection claims about what we can.

### Operator-first design
The SOC analyst using Detec every day is the most important user. Every UI decision, every alert format, every piece of language is optimized for their workflow, not for a demo audience.

## Brand Personality

**Modern & Approachable** — in the territory of Tailscale, Linear, and Datadog. Security that feels like a well-built developer tool, not a threat-hunting war room.

### Personality traits

| Trait | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|---|---|---|
| Precise | Measured language, defensible claims, specific numbers | Cold, clinical, legalistic |
| Honest | Publishes limits, acknowledges gaps, shows uncertainty | Apologetic, underselling, hedging everything |
| Calm | Leads with evidence not urgency, no alarm bells | Passive, disengaged, boring |
| Capable | Deep technical substance, real lab validation | Arrogant, dismissive of alternatives |

## Positioning

### Target audience

| Segment | Role | What they care about |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary buyer** | SOC Manager / Security Ops Lead | Visibility into ungoverned tools, audit trail, proportional enforcement |
| **Technical evaluator** | Security Engineer / IT Admin | Detection accuracy, confidence scoring, SIEM integration, deployment friction |
| **Economic buyer** | CISO / VP Security | Compliance posture, risk reduction, coverage breadth, honest limitations |
| **Internal champion** | DevOps / Platform Engineer | Low friction, no developer disruption for low-risk tools, clear policy logic |

### Positioning statement

Detec discovers and controls autonomous AI tools on developer endpoints. For security teams who need to govern them, Detec is an endpoint agent that detects tools by capability class and enforces policy with confidence-scored, auditable decisions. Unlike application blocklists or manual inventory, Detec uses multi-signal detection across five evidence layers so that new tools map to existing policy automatically and every enforcement decision is explainable.

### Competitive differentiation

| Dimension | Detec's approach |
|---|---|
| Detection method | Multi-signal (5 layers) with per-tool calibrated weights, not binary signatures or name matching |
| Classification | Capability-based classes (A/B/C/D), not product-name lists |
| Decision quality | Confidence-scored (0.00–1.00) with full evidence chain, not yes/no |
| Enforcement | Four-state ladder (Detect/Warn/Approve/Block) driven by policy, not one-size-fits-all |
| Trust model | Published known limits, lab validation results, evasion testing, honest gaps |

## Brand Pillars

The three core themes that anchor all Detec messaging:

### 1. Detection that shows its work
Multi-signal confidence scoring with full evidence chains. Every detection is traceable to specific processes, files, connections, identity markers, and behavioral patterns. No black boxes.

### 2. Governance that fits the risk
The enforcement ladder is policy-driven and proportional. Low-risk tools get visibility. High-risk tools get gates. The operator decides where the friction goes.

### 3. Trust that's earned, not claimed
Known limits are published. Evasion vectors are tested and documented. Lab validation is real, not theoretical. Transparency is a feature.
